# Tournament Rules

This document captures the tournament structure and governance rules translated from the official Spanish regulation.

## Definitions

- Era: A group of consecutive seasons. When an era ends, a new era starts and season numbering resets.
- Season: A time-bounded competition cycle within an era.
- Zone: A group of exactly 20 players within a season. All competitions occur inside a zone.
- Team: Each zone has 4 teams of 5 players (1 captain + 4 members).

## Roster and Zone Formation

- A roster is created before each season. Players must confirm participation.
- The roster determines the final number of zones (must be a multiple of 20 players).
- Only rostered players can compete during the season.

## Team Formation

- Players are split into 4 teams per zone.
- Distribution should aim for competitive balance.
- Once formed, teams remain unchanged for the full season.

## Team Captain

### Selection

- The captain is the player with the highest team score from the previous season.
- A captain whose team is relegated cannot captain the next season.
- The relegated captain is replaced by the Champion of the Champions Cup for the next season.

### Responsibilities

- Coordinate team schedules.
- Represent the team to tournament organizers.
- Select which players participate in each competition.
- Assign Helper and Extreme roles where required.
- Submit a one-time season wager list that includes:
	- Competitions where the team could win (Joker).
	- Competitions where rival teams could finish last (Bomb).
	- Potential protagonists for each competition (Helpers).

## Seasons and Eras

- The tournament runs in numbered seasons (Season 1, 2, 3, etc.).
- An era is composed of multiple consecutive seasons.
- A player can win an era by meeting one or more cumulative goals:
	- Win 3 leagues.
	- Win 3 cups.
	- Be a captain at least once.
	- Finish Top 1 of the clan in a season.

When an era ends, the current cycle closes and season numbering restarts.

## Champions and Relegation

- The Team Cup champion team keeps its structure for the next season.
- The relegated team is fully disbanded.
- In other teams, the captain remains since they are the most senior member.

## Player Ranking and League Placement

- A general ranking from 1 to 48 is determined by:
	- Cups won.
	- Points earned in leagues and competitions.
	- Historical performance.

- This ranking determines draft order and league placement:
	- League A: ranks 1 to 6.
	- League B: ranks 7 to 12.
	- League C: ranks 13 to 20.

This system remains from Season 1 until the final season of an era.

## Official Communication Channels

- The competition is coordinated through official WhatsApp/Discord groups.
- Results channels are organized by zone.
- Official result posts use agreed symbols to indicate roles and penalties.

## Competition Structure

The tournament includes team competitions, team leagues, and individual cups. All of them count for statistics, titles, and progression toward the Champions Cup.

### Team Competitions and Leagues

- Team League: Main daily team competition, played in rounds across the season.
- Team Cup: Aggregated points from all competitions, leagues, and strategic bets.
- Captain League: Cup played in four rounds with captains and assigned helpers.
- Doubles League: Team competition played as best-of-three 2v2 matches.
- Warriors Cup: Cup played during clan war days with captain + helper scoring.

### Individual Cups

- Qualifiers: 20 players, single elimination, best-of-three.
- League Cup: Group stage, semifinals, final, and third-place match.
- Revenge Cup: For qualifier losers, with semifinal and final stages.

### Individual Leagues (A, B, C)

- League A: 6 players.
- League B: 6 players.
- League C: 8 players.
- The competition ends when all matches that award Individual League points have been played.
- The top player in each league is crowned champion.

### Champions Cup

- Played in the final stage of the season.
- Determines the absolute champion.
- Final result can be decided up to a best-of-nine aggregate.
- Winning team is crowned Team Cup Champion.

## Tie Resolution (Best-of Series)

- If a match series ends 4-4, the winner is decided by a doubles match (2v2) played as best-of-three.

## Post-Draft Team Operations

- After the draft, each captain creates an official team WhatsApp group.
- The group name and icon must match the official team identity.
- All rostered players are added to the group.
- The group is the official space for coordination and reminders.

Captains are responsible for ensuring:
- Assigned players complete their full duels on time.
- Players assigned to special competitions (Warriors, Extreme, etc.) comply with deadlines.

## Governance and Dispute Resolution

### Controllers

- Controllers are appointed by the organizers.
- They intervene in conflicts, scheduling disputes, reporting errors, misconduct, and game issues.
- Controllers review evidence and can escalate to VAR.

### VAR (Final Decision)

- VAR is composed of the main organizers.
- VAR makes final, non-appealable decisions.
- VAR can decide on advancement, result corrections, sanctions, and expulsions.

### Match Coordination Conflicts

If players cannot coordinate within the deadline, controllers review:

- Proposed schedules.
- Responses (or lack thereof).
- Intent to play.

VAR may decide:

- Walkover.
- Exceptional reschedule.
- Advancement of one player.

### Disconnections

- If a player disconnects, the opponent may offer a rematch as a gesture.
- If the disconnection is due to connection issues, the opponent is not obligated to replay.
- If no replay occurs, the connected player wins.
- The disconnected player cannot appeal the decision.
