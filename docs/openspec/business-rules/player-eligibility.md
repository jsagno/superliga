# Player Eligibility and Participation

This document defines eligibility, participation, and absence rules translated from the official Spanish regulation.

## Roster Requirements

- A roster is created before each season, and players must confirm participation.
- The roster determines the final number of zones (must be a multiple of 20 players).
- Only rostered players are allowed to compete during the season.

### Roster Publication

- Roster start is announced officially in the results group.
- The announcement includes the word ROSTER in uppercase.

### Player Registration

- Players register on a numbered list in order of signup.
- The list closes once the total count reaches a multiple of 20.

## Participation Requirements

To participate, each player must:

- Remain in the official clan(s) for the entire season.
- Be present in official WhatsApp/Discord groups.
- Maintain active participation in assigned competitions.

Unjustified departure from the clan or official groups is considered a disciplinary fault and can lead to sanctions.

## Inactivity Penalties

Each player must record points daily or per match date, depending on the competition format.

Penalty schedule for inactivity:

- 1st day/date without points: -1 point
- 2nd consecutive day/date without points: -1 additional point and -2 points
- 3rd consecutive day/date without points: -5 points
- 4th consecutive day/date without points: automatic expulsion from the competition

Inactivity applies to both unannounced absence and refusal to play.

## Player Replacement

If a player is expelled or withdraws voluntarily:

- The team captain may replace them with any summoned player, even if:
  - The player was not included in the original roster.
  - The player is not listed as an official substitute (substitutes have priority first).
- The replacement player must have competitive characteristics similar to the departing player, subject to organizer review.

## Replacement Time Limit

- Captains may replace players only until 50% of the competition has been completed.
- After that limit:
  - No new players may join.
  - The absent player is replaced by a team Bot.

## Bot Rules (Absences)

### Bot Definition

- A Bot is used when a player leaves after 50% of the competition and can no longer be replaced.

### Bot Scoring by Competition

- Daily duel: the Bot scores the average of the other four team members.
  - If more than one Bot is active, use the lowest score.
- League Cup and Revenge Cup: the opponent wins automatically 2-1.
- Warriors Cup: the Bot scores half of the helper points.
- Doubles League: Bots cannot participate.

### Temporary Absence

- A player may be temporarily absent for travel or force majeure.
- The captain must notify the day before the absence (until further notice).
- The player is treated as a Bot and Bot rules apply.

### Permanent Absence

- If a player leaves before half of the competition (before Round 2 ends), they may be replaced by a new player who did not participate that season.
- The replacement:
  - Cannot play League Cup or Revenge Cup (their matches are treated as Bot matches).
  - Inherits 50% of the replaced player's league points.
- The replaced player is permanently removed for the season.

### Max Bots per Team

- Each team may have up to three active Bots during a season.
- Upon reaching a fourth Bot, the team is automatically:
  - Disqualified from the competition.
  - Relegated, regardless of position in any competition.
- This disqualification is automatic and final.

## Deadlines and Discipline

- All competitions have defined deadlines.
- Failing to play within the required time without prior notice is sanctioned.
- Sanctions may include:
  - Loss of points or cups.
  - Suspension from competitions.
  - Removal from the roster.

## Conduct and Disciplinary Actions

Sanctionable conduct includes:

- Misconduct or disrespectful behavior.
- Insults or offensive language.
- Repeated conflicts.
- Deliberate noncompliance with the rules.

### Incorrect or False Results

- Incorrect result submissions are reviewed by controllers and escalated to VAR if needed.

### Sanction Escalation

1. First warning:
  - Correct the result or apply a -1 penalty.
2. Second warning:
  - Result becomes -2.
  - May include direct expulsion.
3. Third level:
  - Apply a -5 penalty.
4. Final step:
  - Permanent expulsion from the competition.

Severe repeat offenses may trigger immediate expulsion without all steps.

## Bad Manners (BM) Preference

- Players can declare whether they accept BM (emote taunts) during games.
- A white flag indicates the player does not accept BM.
- If a player with a white flag receives BM, they can request a review.
- Organizers verify incidents using Royale API.

### BM Sanctions

- Unauthorized BM against a white-flag player can result in disciplinary action.
- Sanctions range from warnings to penalties or expulsion depending on severity or repeat offense.

## Acceptance of Rules

- Enrolling in the roster implies full acceptance of the regulations and annexes.
- Ignorance of the rules is not a valid excuse.
- The regulation takes effect with roster publication and remains active for the season and era.
